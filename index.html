About Us

The Wai Wong Research Group conducts research on transportation engineering, traffic flow theory, intelligent transportation systems, connected and automated vehicles, and transport safety.

Our work combines mathematical modelling, simulation, data-driven analysis, and empirical validation to understand and improve the performance, reliability, and safety of transportation systems.

Research focus: modelling, estimation, and evaluation of emerging transportation systems under uncertainty, partial observability, and mixed traffic conditions.

Research Areas

Traffic Flow Theory and Modelling

We study the dynamics of traffic flow using analytical models, simulation, and empirical data, with particular interest in congestion formation, queue evolution, and network performance.

Connected and Automated Vehicles

We investigate how connected and automated vehicles can improve traffic observability, safety, efficiency, and control under mixed traffic environments.

Signalized Intersection Operations

We develop methods for estimating, reconstructing, and evaluating traffic states at signalized intersections using partial observations and emerging sensing technologies.

Traffic Safety and Human Factors

We examine driving behaviour, interaction patterns, lane-changing behaviour, and safety-related performance measures in complex traffic environments.

Data-driven Transportation Systems

We use trajectory data, simulation outputs, connected vehicle observations, and statistical learning methods to support transportation modelling and evaluation.
