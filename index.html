About the Group

The Wai Wong Research Group develops analytical, statistical, simulation-based, and data-driven methods for next-generation transportation systems.

The group focuses on smart transportation systems enabled by emerging sensing, connectivity, and automation technologies. Its research addresses traffic monitoring, connected and autonomous vehicles, traffic flow theory, traffic signal optimization, emergency response and evacuation, and sustainable transport.

Research Themes

The group works across transportation engineering, data analytics, traffic operations, safety, and intelligent transportation systems.

Big data analytics Connected and autonomous vehicles Traffic flow theory Signal optimization Intelligent transportation systems Emergency response and evacuation Sustainable transport

Connected and Autonomous Vehicles

We study how vehicle connectivity and automation can support traffic monitoring, state estimation, signal control, safety assessment, and network operations.

Traffic Flow Theory

We develop and evaluate models for traffic dynamics, congestion, queue formation, macroscopic traffic relationships, and network-level traffic performance.

Signal Optimization

We investigate adaptive and stochastic signal control methods under uncertain, partially observed, and connected-vehicle-supported traffic environments.

Safety, Evacuation, and Resilience

We examine transport safety, emergency response, evacuation operations, and system resilience using empirical data, modelling, and emerging sensing technologies.
