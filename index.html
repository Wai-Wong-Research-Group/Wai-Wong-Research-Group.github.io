The Wai Wong Research Group conducts research at the intersection of transportation engineering and emerging transport technologies. The group focuses on smart transportation systems, connected and automated vehicles, big data analytics, intelligent transportation systems, traffic signal optimisation, traffic flow theory, and safety science.

Our work combines mathematical modelling, simulation, empirical data analysis, and statistical estimation to support the development of reliable, scalable, and safety-conscious solutions for next-generation transportation systems.

Research focus: modelling, estimation, optimisation, and evaluation of transportation systems under uncertainty, partial observability, and mixed traffic conditions.

Research Areas

The group works on both theoretical and applied problems in transportation engineering, with particular emphasis on emerging sensing technologies, connected vehicle data, traffic operations, and safety evaluation.

Connected and automated vehicles Traffic flow theory Intelligent transportation systems Traffic signal optimisation Big data analytics Safety science Probe vehicle data Signalized intersections

Connected and Automated Vehicles

We study how connected and automated vehicles can improve traffic observability, operational efficiency, and safety in mixed traffic systems.

Traffic Flow Theory

We develop and evaluate models of traffic flow dynamics, congestion formation, queue evolution, and network-level traffic performance.

Traffic Signal Optimisation

We investigate signal control and optimisation methods for intersections and urban traffic networks under uncertain and partially observed traffic conditions.

Traffic Safety and Human Factors

We examine driving behaviour, interaction patterns, safety risks, and performance measures using trajectory data and statistical modelling.
