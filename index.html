Wai Wong Research Group

Department of Civil and Environmental Engineering, University of Canterbury, New Zealand

The Wai Wong Research Group conducts research on smart transportation systems, connected and automated vehicles, big data analytics, intelligent transportation systems, traffic signal optimisation, traffic flow theory, emergency response and evacuation, and sustainable transport.

The group develops analytical, statistical, simulation-based, and data-driven methods to understand and improve the performance, reliability, resilience, and safety of transportation systems under emerging sensing, connectivity, and automation technologies.

Research focus: modelling, estimation, optimisation, and evaluation of transportation systems under uncertainty, partial observability, and mixed traffic conditions.

Research Interests

The group works on both theoretical and applied problems in transportation engineering, with a focus on emerging transport technologies and their implications for traffic operations, safety, control, monitoring, and system resilience.

Big data analytics Connected and autonomous vehicles Traffic flow theory Signal optimization Intelligent transportation systems Emergency response and evacuation Sustainable transport

Connected and Autonomous Vehicles

We study how vehicle connectivity and automation can improve traffic observability, state estimation, signal control, safety, and network operations in mixed traffic systems.

Traffic Flow Theory

We develop and evaluate models of traffic flow dynamics, congestion formation, queue evolution, and network-level traffic performance.

Signal Optimization

We investigate traffic signal control and optimisation methods under uncertain, partially observed, and connected-vehicle-supported traffic environments.

Transport Safety and Resilience

We examine transportation safety, emergency response, evacuation, and resilience using empirical data, modelling, and emerging sensing technologies.
